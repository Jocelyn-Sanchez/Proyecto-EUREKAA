Magnetic Materials and Their Properties

Introduction

Magnetic materials are substances whose electronic structure allows interaction with external magnetic fields. Their behavior depends on the alignment and response of the atomic magnetic moments, which determines whether the material is ferromagnetic, paramagnetic, diamagnetic, ferrimagnetic, or antiferromagnetic.

Classification of Magnetic Materials

Ferromagnetic

They present a strong alignment of magnetic domains. They are used in permanent magnets, motors, transformers, and electronic devices. Iron, nickel, and cobalt are the main examples.

Paramagnetic

They respond weakly to magnetic fields due to the partial orientation of their unpaired electrons. Their magnetization is proportional to the intensity of the external field.

Diamagnetic

They oppose magnetic fields by generating induced fields in the opposite direction. All materials possess some degree of diamagnetism, although it is usually very weak.

Ferrimagnetic

They have opposing magnetic moments that do not completely cancel out. Ferrites—widely used in antennas, inductors, and magnetic memory—belong to this category.

Antiferromagnetic

Their atomic magnetic moments align in opposite directions, almost completely canceling each other. They are important in advanced research and spintronics technologies.
