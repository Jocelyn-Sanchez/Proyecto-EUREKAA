Magnetism on Earth and in Space

The Earth's Magnetic Field

Generated by the movement of liquid iron in the outer core, Earth’s natural dynamo creates a protective field that deflects most of the solar wind and keeps the atmosphere stable. Without this field, solar radiation would erode the atmospheric layer.

The Magnetosphere

It is the region dominated by Earth's field where charged particles from the Sun interact. It regulates geomagnetic storms, auroras, and magnetic reconnection phenomena.

Magnetism on the Sun

The Sun possesses highly dynamic fields that produce sunspots, solar flares, coronal mass ejections, and complex coronal structures. These emissions can affect satellites, power grids, and communications on Earth.

Magnetism in Interstellar Space

Galaxies and nebulae also contain magnetic fields that influence star formation and the behavior of interstellar plasma. Their study is key to understanding large-scale cosmological processes.
